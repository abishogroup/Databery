Almost every B2B team has a target account list. Almost none of them use it well. It gets built in a burst of enthusiasm, lives in a spreadsheet, and quietly dies as reps drift back to whatever's in front of them. The list isn't the problem — the way it's built is.

## Why most lists fail

Two reasons. First, they're built on firmographics alone — "companies of this size, in this industry" — which produces a list that's technically correct and completely uninspiring to work. Second, they hand the rep a name and nothing else. Faced with a bare company name and no context, a rep will always choose the warmer, easier task.

> A target list isn't a filter result. It's a set of decisions about where your team should spend its most valuable hours.

## Move 1: Score for fit and timing, separately

Fit ("should we ever sell to them?") and timing ("should we sell to them now?") are different questions, and collapsing them is why good accounts get ignored. Score them on separate axes. A perfect-fit account with no timing signal goes on the nurture track; a strong-fit account that just lit up goes to the top of the queue.

## Move 2: Attach a reason to every account

This is the move that changes everything. Every account on the list should carry a one-line answer to "why is this here, and why now?" — a trigger, a signal, a relevant change. A reason turns a cold name into a warm reason to reach out.

## Move 3: Keep it small enough to be real

- A list of 500 "targets" is a database, not a plan.
- 20–30 researched accounts per rep is something a human can actually work.
- It's better to deeply work a short list than to lightly spam a long one.

## Move 4: Make it a living thing

A target list built once is stale within a quarter. Leadership changes, new hires, funding — the signals that made an account interesting (or not) shift constantly. The lists that get used are the ones that get refreshed, with new accounts promoted in and quiet ones rotated out.

## The payoff

When a list is fit-and-timing scored, carries a reason per account, stays short, and stays current, something changes on the floor: reps open it. It stops being an aspirational artifact and becomes the first place they look each morning.
